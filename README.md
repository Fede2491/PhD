Extensive study of Electroweak corrections up to one-loop in perturbation theory. 

The main goal of the thesis is to compute a certain number of pseudo-observables (POs) in Standard Model Effective Field Theory  (SMEFT) and study how large are the deviation from Standard Model predictions, once the SMEFT scale and the range of Wilson coefficients are selected. 
Working in an equiprobable space of Wilson coefficients, we have shown that for
different scenarios the deviations from the SM are generally < 10%, with the largest
fraction of them compatibles with zero. By studying the correlation among POs,
we have not observed specific trends. However, putting constraints on the Wilson
coefficients and analyzing the scattered plots, we have been able to explain how the
magnitude of deviations varies with respect to various POs, remarking the importance of non-negligible contribution to one-loop corrections.
